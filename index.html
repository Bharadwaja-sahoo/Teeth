Teeth are essential structures in the human body that help in chewing food, speaking clearly, and maintaining facial structure. They are made of enamel, the hardest substance in the body, and are categorized into different types, including incisors, canines, premolars, and molars. Each type serves a specific function in breaking down food.

The human mouth contains two sets of teeth in a lifetime: primary (baby) teeth and permanent teeth. The primary teeth start appearing around six months of age and are eventually replaced by permanent teeth. A full set of adult teeth consists of 32 teeth, including wisdom teeth.

Good oral hygiene is crucial for maintaining healthy teeth. Brushing twice daily, flossing, and regular dental check-ups help prevent cavities, gum diseases, and other dental problems. Poor oral hygiene can lead to tooth decay, infections, and even systemic health issues.

Teeth can suffer from various problems like cavities, gum disease, and misalignment. Treatments such as fillings, braces, and root canals help in managing these issues. In severe cases, missing teeth can be replaced with dental implants or dentures.

Overall, teeth play a significant role in digestion and overall health. Proper dental care ensures strong teeth and a confident smile. Regular visits to the dentist and a balanced diet contribute to maintaining long-lasting oral health.
